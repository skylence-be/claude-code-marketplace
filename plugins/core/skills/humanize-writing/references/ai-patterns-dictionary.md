## Structural Patterns

| Pattern | Tell | Fix |
|---------|------|-----|
| **Em dashes** | 3 to 5x human rate ("ChatGPT dash") | Never. Replace every one with a comma, period, or parentheses. |
| **Parallel negation** | "Not X, but Y" — 5–10× more than human text | State the positive directly. |
| **Rule of three** | Forces trios to sound comprehensive | Pick the one or two that matter. |
| **Rhetorical Q + answer** | "What does this mean? It means..." | Just say it. |
| **Mirror structure** | Consecutive sentences with identical shape | Break the symmetry; vary length and angle. |
| **Neat endings** | Every paragraph wraps up with a takeaway | Let ≥30% just stop. |
| **Inflation of importance** | "pivotal moment", "testament to", "crucial development" | Delete. State facts; let the reader judge. |
| **Signposting** | "Let's dive in", "Here's what you need to know" | Drop it; start with the substance. |
| **Fragmented headers** | Heading → one-liner restating it → real content | Delete the one-liner. |
| **Inline-header lists** | Every bullet: **Bold term:** explanation | Prose or plain bullets instead. |
| **Copula avoidance** | "serves as", "stands as", "boasts" instead of "is"/"has" | Use "is", "are", "has". |
| **Superficial -ing clauses** | "...reflecting X, symbolizing Y, showcasing Z" | One concrete claim with a source. |
| **Vague attributions** | "Experts believe", "Studies show" | Name the source or drop the claim. |
| **Formulaic challenges** | "Despite challenges... continues to thrive" | State the actual problem and what happened. |

## Communication Artifacts (cut entirely)

"Great question!" · "Certainly!" · "You're absolutely right!" · "I hope this helps!" · "Let me know if you'd like me to expand" · "As of my last training..." · "While specific details are limited..." · "Here is an overview of..."

## Formatting Tells

- **Boldface overuse** — mechanical emphasis on every other phrase
- **Emojis in professional text** — 🚀💡✅ on bullets and headings
- **Title Case In Every Heading** → sentence case
- **Curly quotes** "..." → straight quotes "..."
- **Hyphenated pairs** — AI is perfectly consistent ("data-driven", "client-facing"); humans aren't

---

## Tone Tells

- Uniform sentence length — AI is metronomic; vary short/long/fragment
- Too balanced — AI gives equal weight to all sides; humans have opinions
- Excessive hedging — "generally speaking", "it could be argued"; just say it
- No concrete details — replace vague claims with names, numbers, dates
- Generic conclusions — "The future looks bright"; end with something specific
